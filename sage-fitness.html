The philosophy

We built the opposite of a gym floor. No crowds. No noise. No waiting for a bench.

Most fitness businesses grow by filling floor space. We grow by refusing to. Every session — coached or autonomous — happens on a floor that has never once held more than four people at a time.

That's not a marketing number. It's a hard operational cap, held across appointment scheduling and membership sizing alike, in service of clinical-grade safety, undivided coaching attention, and a training environment built for longevity — not a scene.

The founder

founder portrait — Dr. Sage Noh
three-quarter frame, natural light, facility backdrop

Dr. Sage Noh

"109 pounds in 2000. 200 pounds, under 10% body fat, by 2007. Not by accident — by sports science, applied deliberately, for seven years."

Academic path

NYU, 2004 — Music Composition & Theory, completed alongside full pre-med prerequisites and a Chemistry minor. Advanced into USC's Doctorate track for Physical Therapy.

Trained at

Gravity Spa at Le Parker Méridien Hotel, Trump Riverside, Palladium Gym, and World Gym — New York City's top luxury fitness environments.

The transformation

Dr. Noh's own body is the proof of concept behind the practice. A seven-year rebuild — from 109 lbs to 200 lbs at under 10% body fat — grounded not in willpower alone, but in the deliberate application of biomechanics and sports science. Every program at Sage Fitness carries that same discipline: measured, sequenced, and built to last.
